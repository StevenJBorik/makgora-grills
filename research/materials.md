## 1. GOLD ALLOYS

### Overview
Gold has been the traditional material for dental grillz since the 1980s hip-hop era. Pure 24K gold is too soft for oral applications, so alloys are standard.

### Karat Options

| Karat | Gold % | Hardness (Vickers) | Pros | Cons |
|-------|--------|-------------------|------|------|
| **10K** | 41.7% | 150-180 HV | Durable, affordable | Less premium, can tarnish |
| **14K** | 58.3% | 130-160 HV | Balance of durability + color | Standard choice |
| **18K** | 75.0% | 120-140 HV | Rich color, hypoallergenic | Softer, more expensive |
| **22K** | 91.6% | 80-100 HV | Ultra-premium look | Too soft for daily wear |

### Color Variations
- **Yellow Gold:** Classic, achieved with silver/copper alloy
- **White Gold:** Nickel or palladium alloy (NOTE: nickel can cause allergies)
- **Rose Gold:** Higher copper content
- **Green Gold:** Silver-heavy alloy

### Manufacturing Methods
1. **Lost Wax Casting** (traditional)
   - Create wax model from dental impression
   - Encase in investment plaster
   - Burn out wax, pour molten gold
   - Polish and finish
   
2. **CAD/CAM + 3D Print Casting**
   - Design in CAD (Zoo CAD)
   - 3D print castable resin pattern
   - Lost wax cast from resin pattern
   - Finish

### Pricing Reference (Market 2026)
- **Basic 2-tooth 10K:** $200-400
- **6-tooth 14K set:** $800-1,500
- **Full set 18K + diamonds:** $3,000-15,000+
- **Celebrity custom (Johnny Dang tier):** $30,000-1,000,000+

### Suppliers
- **Rio Grande** (riogrand.com) – Casting grain, alloys
- **Stuller** (stuller.com) – Precious metals, findings
- **Hoover & Strong** – Recycled gold options

## 2. TITANIUM

### Overview
Titanium is the *ideal* material for OrcGrills™ comfort philosophy due to its exceptional strength-to-weight ratio and biocompatibility. It's the same material used in medical implants.

### Grades

| Grade | Composition | Tensile Strength | Best For |
|-------|-------------|------------------|----------|
| **Grade 2 (CP)** | Pure titanium | 345 MPa | Simple designs |
| **Grade 5 (Ti-6Al-4V)** | Alloy with aluminum/vanadium | 895 MPa | Complex geometry, thin walls |
| **Grade 23 (ELI)** | Medical grade | 860 MPa | Implant-grade, premium |

### Anodization for Color
Titanium can be anodized to produce a full spectrum of colors through oxide layer thickness manipulation:

| Voltage | Color | Use Case |
|---------|-------|----------|
| 18-22V | Gold/Yellow | Imperial War Jade accent |
| 25-35V | Purple/Blue | Void Jade, Fel themes |
| 40-50V | Cyan/Teal | Loa Bone Turquoise |
| 60-70V | Pink/Magenta | Custom |
| 80-90V | Green | Jade family themes |

> **Note:** Anodized colors are permanent and biocompatible – no coatings to chip.

### Manufacturing Methods
1. **CNC Milling** (preferred for grillz)
   - Direct machining from titanium billet
   - Tolerances: ±0.05mm achievable
   - Excellent for thin-wall designs
   
2. **SLM/DMLS 3D Printing**
   - Direct metal laser sintering
   - Complex geometries possible
   - Requires post-processing (polishing)
   
3. **Investment Casting**
   - Similar to gold casting
   - Requires inert atmosphere (argon)
   - More challenging than gold

### Cost Estimate
- **Material:** $15-30/oz (vs $2,000+/oz for gold)
- **CNC machining:** $50-150 per piece (setup + labor)
- **Anodization:** $10-30 per piece
- **Total per grill:** $100-300 (vs $500-2,000+ for gold)

### Suppliers
- **Titanium Industries** (titanium.com)
- **United Titanium** (unitedtitanium.com)
- **Online Metals** (onlinemetals.com) – Small quantities

## 3. SURGICAL STEEL 316L

### Overview
The budget option. 316L stainless steel is the same material used in body piercings and basic surgical instruments. Safe for oral use.

### Properties
- **Composition:** Iron, 16-18% chromium, 10-14% nickel, 2-3% molybdenum
- **Hardness:** 170-220 HV
- **Corrosion resistance:** Excellent (but not as good as titanium)
- **Weight:** ~8 g/cm³ (heavier than titanium at 4.5 g/cm³)

### Color Options (PVD Coating)
Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD) coatings can add color:
- Gold tone (titanium nitride)
- Black (titanium carbonitride)
- Rose gold (titanium aluminum nitride)
- Rainbow (multi-layer)

> **Warning:** PVD coatings can wear over time with oral exposure. Not ideal for daily wear.

### Manufacturing
- **CNC Milling:** Standard approach
- **Investment Casting:** Straightforward
- **MIM (Metal Injection Molding):** For high-volume production

### Cost Estimate
- **Material:** Very low ($2-5/piece)
- **Manufacturing:** $30-80 per piece
- **PVD coating:** $15-40
- **Total:** $50-150

### Use Recommendation
✅ Entry-level "Warbringer Starter" line
✅ Festival/cosplay grillz
❌ Not recommended for premium comfort line

## 4. DENTAL RESINS (3D PRINTED)

### Overview
For prototyping, cosplay, and lower-cost options. Modern biocompatible resins are safe for short-term oral contact.

### Recommended Resins

| Product | Manufacturer | Biocompatibility | Durability | Color |
|---------|--------------|------------------|------------|-------|
| **Dental LT Clear V2** | Formlabs | Class IIa (long-term) | High | Clear, tintable |
| **Dental SG** | Formlabs | Class I (short-term) | Medium | Clear |
| **NextDent Ortho Flex** | 3D Systems | Class IIa | High | Pink/Clear |
| **KeySplint Soft** | Keystone | Class IIa | Very High | Clear |
| **SprintRay Crown** | SprintRay | Class IIa | Very High | Tooth shades |

### Post-Processing Requirements
1. **Wash** in IPA (isopropyl alcohol) – 10-15 min
2. **Cure** under UV (405nm) – 30-60 min at 60°C
3. **Polish** for smooth finish
4. **Optional:** Apply biocompatible clear coat

### Color Application
- **Pre-mixed pigmented resins:** Limited color options
- **Custom tinting:** Add pigment to clear resin (test biocompatibility!)
- **Post-print painting:** Use biocompatible paints only
- **UV-reactive:** Available for "glow" effects (Void Jade concept)

### Cost Estimate
- **Resin:** $150-300/L (roughly $3-8 per grill)
- **Printing:** $5-15 (time + energy)
- **Post-processing:** $5-10
- **Total:** $15-35 per piece

### Use Recommendation
✅ Prototyping before metal production
✅ Customer try-on samples
✅ Budget "Cosplay Fang" line
✅ UV-reactive special editions
❌ Not for premium daily-wear products

## 5. ZIRCONIA (CERAMIC)

### Overview
Zirconia (zirconium dioxide, ZrO₂) is used in dental crowns and bridges. Extremely biocompatible and natural-looking, but limited color options.

### Properties
- **Hardness:** 1200-1400 HV (very hard)
- **Density:** 6.0 g/cm³
- **Color:** White, pre-shaded to tooth colors
- **Translucency:** Can be tuned for natural tooth appearance

### Limitations for OrcGrills™
- ❌ Cannot achieve metallic jade/gold looks
- ❌ Brittle – not ideal for thin tusk geometry
- ❌ Limited to natural tooth colors
- ✅ Could work for "White Jade Bone" archetype if combined with metal accents

### Manufacturing
- **CAD/CAM Milling:** Standard in dental labs
- **Sintering:** Required after milling (1500°C)

### Cost
- **Milling + sintering:** $50-150 per unit
- Comparable to titanium

## 6. HYBRID CONSTRUCTION (RECOMMENDED)

### OrcGrills™ Optimal Material Strategy

For your comfort-focused design philosophy, I recommend a **hybrid approach**:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    OUTER SHELL                          │
│         (Visual layer - what the eye sees)              │
│   Material: Anodized Titanium Grade 5 OR Gold 14K       │
│   Thickness: 0.4-0.6mm                                  │
│   Purpose: Aesthetics, color, durability                │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                    INNER LINER                          │
│         (Contact layer - what the tooth feels)          │
│   Material: Soft biocompatible silicone OR resin        │
│   Thickness: 0.2-0.4mm                                  │
│   Purpose: Comfort, cushioning, retention               │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Benefits of Hybrid
1. **Weight reduction:** Hollow metal shell = lighter
2. **Comfort:** Soft liner eliminates pressure points
3. **Retention:** Silicone grips without undercuts
4. **Thermal insulation:** Liner protects from hot/cold metal
5. **Adjustability:** Liner can be relined for fit tweaks

### Soft Liner Materials
- **Sil-Soft (Shore A 40):** Medical-grade silicone
- **Molloplast-B:** Dental lab standard
- **Flexacryl Soft:** Easier to apply

## 8. SAFETY & COMPLIANCE

### Required Standards
- **ISO 10993:** Biological evaluation of medical devices
- **ISO 22674:** Metallic materials for dental restorations
- **FDA Class I:** General dental devices (no 510(k) required for grillz)

### Testing Recommendations
- **Cytotoxicity:** Required for any skin/mouth contact
- **Sensitization:** Especially for nickel-containing alloys
- **Corrosion:** Salt spray testing for metal finishes

### Warnings to Include
- Not a medical device
- Remove before eating
- Clean daily with non-abrasive solution
- Consult dentist if discomfort occurs
